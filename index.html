KAMDANI

Awadh-e-Karigari

Kamdani is essentially a metal work technique. A flattened metallic wire, called ‘badla taar’ is pierced through the cloth or threaded with a needle and twisted to create patterns. The origin of this craft is also ascribed to the harems of Awadh’s Nawabs, where a seamstress from Murshirabad embroidered a cap for the Nawab to please him. Jealous of the attention she received from the king, other inmates of the harem followed her and thus evolved the art of Kamdani.Over time, there are several stitches of cikankari that were absorbed into the work of kamdani, which gives the elegance of chikankari with the richness of badla. Kamdani work also contains some flower motif of jamdani craft and jali of chikankari.
